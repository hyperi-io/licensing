## 2. Limitation of liability where the ACL permits

Licensed Work is not of a kind ordinarily acquired for personal, domestic or
household use or consumption, the Licensor's liability for failure to comply
with a consumer guarantee that cannot lawfully be excluded is limited, at the
Licensor's option, to:

  (a) for goods: replacing the goods or supplying equivalent goods, repairing
      the goods, or paying the cost of replacing the goods or of having the
      goods repaired; and

  (b) for services: supplying the services again, or paying the cost of having
      the services supplied again,

as permitted by section 64A of the ACL. This limitation does not apply if you
establish that it is not fair or reasonable for the Licensor to rely on it.

> Notes on clause 2:
> - Chapeau and (a)/(b): [BORROWED] -- the goods and services options track
>   ACL s64A(1) and s64A(2) in substance (s64A wording verified primary; see
>   the dossier Part 2.2). "not of a kind ordinarily acquired for personal,
>   domestic or household use or consumption" is the s64A statutory gateway.

>   [BORROWED] -- tracks s64A(3). The statute says "fair OR reasonable" (not
