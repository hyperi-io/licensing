## 1. Definitions

In this Annexure:

**"Affiliate"** of an entity means any other entity that, directly or
indirectly, Controls, is Controlled by, or is under common Control with that
entity, for so long as that Control subsists.

**"Control"** means holding, directly or indirectly, more than fifty percent
(50%) of the voting securities, voting equity interests, or other voting
ownership of an entity, or otherwise having the power to direct or cause the
direction of the management and policies of that entity, whether by contract
or otherwise.

> Notes on clause 1:
> - "Affiliate" / common-control framing: [BORROWED] in structure from the
>   common-control affiliate machinery in the Elastic License 2.0 and the
>   PolyForm family ("all organizations that have control over, are under the
>   control of, or are under common control with that organization"). HyperI
>   does NOT adopt those licences' definitions verbatim; this clause restates
>   the concept in HyperI's own words.
> - The numeric ">50% voting" threshold for "Control" is [HYPERI-NOVEL].
>   The Elastic/PolyForm definitions of "control" do not fix a numeric
>   percentage ("ownership of substantially all the assets ... or the power to
>   direct its management and policies by vote, contract, or otherwise").
>   HyperI's choice of a bright-line >50% test is a deliberate addition;
>   counsel must confirm the threshold and whether the catch-all "or otherwise
>   having the power to direct ..." should remain alongside the bright line or
>   be removed in favour of the bright line alone.

## 2. Contractors and agents acting on your behalf

For the purposes of the Additional Use Grant, a contractor or agent that
accesses the functionality of the Licensed Work solely on your behalf and for
your own internal purposes, and not on its own account or for the benefit of
any third party, is treated as one of "your employees and contractors" and is
not a third party to whom a Hosted Service is provided.

> Notes on clause 2:
> - [BORROWED / HYPERI-NOVEL hybrid]. The phrase "your employees and
>   contractors" is the verbatim carve-out from the CockroachDB-derived
>   Additional Use Grant in the License. This clause CLARIFIES that carve-out;
>   it does not narrow it. The "solely on your behalf ... not on its own
>   account or for the benefit of any third party" qualifier is [HYPERI-NOVEL]
>   and mirrors the operate-versus-benefit distinction in HyperI's existing
>   COMMERCIAL.md. Counsel to confirm this clarification does not itself read
>   as an additional restriction (BSL Covenant 2).

## 3. Independent use by Affiliates is internal use

Where two or more entities that are Affiliates of one another each deploy and
operate their own instance of the Licensed Work, each for that entity's own
internal purposes, each such entity's use is internal use and is not the
provision of a Hosted Service to a third party. Each such entity exercises the
rights granted by the License in its own right.

> Notes on clause 3:
> - [BORROWED] from HyperI's existing COMMERCIAL.md ("If multiple entities in
>   your corporate group each deploy and operate their own instance of the
>   software for their own internal purposes, each entity may do so ... without
>   a commercial license"). Restated for the BSL/Hosted-Service model. This is
>   a clarifying permission, not a restriction. Counsel to confirm.

## 4. One Affiliate operating for others is a Hosted Service (clarification, not a new restriction)

For the avoidance of doubt, where one entity operates the Licensed Work and
provides access to its functionality to one or more of its Affiliates as a
hosted or managed service (rather than each Affiliate operating its own
instance for its own internal purposes), that operation is the provision of a
Hosted Service within the meaning of the Additional Use Grant, and falls
outside that Grant. A commercial license is required for that use. See
COMMERCIAL.md.

> Notes on clause 4:
> - [BORROWED] from HyperI's existing COMMERCIAL.md ("When Entity A operates
>   software for the benefit of Entity B, that is providing a service --
>   regardless of corporate relationship"). This clause states a consequence
>   of the Additional Use Grant's own boundary (a "Hosted Service" allows
>   "third parties ... to access the functionality"); it does NOT add a new
>   prohibition. An Affiliate is a separate legal entity and therefore a third
>   party for this purpose. [HYPERI-NOVEL FLAG]: counsel must confirm that
>   treating an Affiliate as a "third party" for the Hosted-Service test is
>   the intended reading and is consistent with clause 2 (contractors/agents
>   acting on your behalf are NOT third parties) and clause 3 (independent
>   Affiliate use is internal). The line HyperI draws is operate-versus-
>   benefit, not corporate-relationship.
